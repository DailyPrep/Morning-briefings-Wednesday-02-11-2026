Yesterday's Impact

NFP Analysis

The January jobs report was a masterclass in market interpretation. On paper, it was hawkish: 130K payrolls (2x estimates), unemployment down to 4.3%, and average hourly earnings at 0.4% m/m. The initial reaction was chaos — ES whipsawed 60 points in 15 minutes as algorithms parsed the headline.

But the session close told the real story. Markets chose to focus on the composition of job growth rather than the headline. The bulk of hiring came in lower-wage sectors (leisure, hospitality, retail), suggesting the wage pressure isn't broad-based. This allowed the "soft landing" narrative to survive intact.

Gold's strong move was the standout. While equities rallied on growth optimism, gold rallied on mixed safe-haven/inflation flows. Silver's spike was gamma-driven and has continued with momentum today, as positioning adjusts.
