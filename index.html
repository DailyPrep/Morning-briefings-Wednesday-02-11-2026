Market Vibe

Sentiment

The overnight session is telling a clear story: risk-on is back. ES futures have held the bulk of yesterday's NFP gains, trading at 7,001 — just 14 points off the overnight high. This isn't short-covering; it's genuine accumulation. The VIX compression to 17.81 confirms that fear has evaporated.

What's remarkable is the correlation breakdown between yields and equities. The 10-year is surging (+14bps to 4.48%) yet ES continues to bid. This is the market pricing in growth recovery rather than Fed panic. Real rates are rising because growth expectations are rising — a fundamentally different dynamic than 2022's inflation scare.

Key Context: The DXY is flat at 96.96 despite the risk-on tone. This suggests the dollar isn't being dumped as a funding currency for risk assets — a healthier rally than the "dollar down, everything up" regime of late 2024.

Precious metals are seeing the expected profit-taking after yesterday's explosive moves. Gold gave back $9 overnight but holds above $2,900 — a constructive pullback in an uptrend. Silver is the concern, down 1.33% and breaking below key support at $32.80. The gamma squeeze that drove it to $33.50 yesterday is unwinding fast.

Yesterday's Impact

NFP Analysis

The January jobs report was a masterclass in market interpretation. On paper, it was hawkish: 130K payrolls (2x estimates), unemployment down to 4.3%, and average hourly earnings at 0.4% m/m. The initial reaction was chaos — ES whipsawed 60 points in 15 minutes as algorithms parsed the headline.

But the session close told the real story. Markets chose to focus on the composition of job growth rather than the headline. The bulk of hiring came in lower-wage sectors (leisure, hospitality, retail), suggesting the wage pressure isn't broad-based. This allowed the "soft landing" narrative to survive intact.

Gold's +$48 move to $2,920 was the standout divergence. While equities rallied on growth optimism, gold rallied on safe-haven flows — likely institutional repositioning given geopolitical tensions. Silver's +4.3% spike was largely gamma-driven and is already mean-reverting this morning, as our Research Desk anticipated.

Watch Fed speak for any hawkish pushback, but the market has already made its decision on the jobs data.
